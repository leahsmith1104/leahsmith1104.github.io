Bingo Arcade

Client: Sky Betting and Gaming - Sky Bingo is one of the biggest UK bingo sites, with a large customer base. At Sky Bingo a little bit more excitement is added to their players day. And it’s not just with new and exclusive online bingo - there are also exciting slots and instant win games to enjoy. players can chat to other players; friends and chat room hosts to make their experience more entertaining and engaging.

Project: Sky Bingo Arcade

As part of a business need to create a destination for slot customers on Sky Bingo, extending the current slots offering while not alienating bingo customers, the business brief was put together to create a solution to building and advertising this new destination.

A team was created to tackle this brief which included myself representing UX/UI and Interaction, Insights, Marketing, Brand, Creative, Product, Compliance, Media.

The solution would need an identity piece which would resonate with the customers. In the first design sprint, in which all the brief team attended, it was decided via concept sketching that a window into the arcade page on the homepage would be the best way to intrigue customers onto the page that showcases Arcade.

My role was to focus on the UX/UI. The main requirement for me was to highlight arcade on the homepage, showcasing the games and also the branding and creative assets that would be supplied, to back to media campaign.

Wireframes and user flow

As time was of the essence, the first stage of testing was done internally using wireframes and to varify the user flow. The final idea that came out of the initial design sprint was to have a ‘window’ into arcade from the homepage.

The content in the window should have some emotive language in from the marketing campaigns but also give a teaser to the customer of what games lie beneath. The call to action would take customers to the arcade page itself.

The arcade page then would house all the content and features that slots players would like, including the marketing campaign promotions and daily free to plays. The games should be displayed so that it is easy to discover new content and for customers to find their games.

Applying the branding

The final logo was provided by brand and aimed to carry through the ‘fun’ of Arcade, to capture the eye of any user visiting Bingo, and maybe tempt them into the arcade to play slots. It needed to have the same effect when used on external media, such as TV and Social Media.

Brand also continued the use of the ‘burst’ which allows the current branding used for Bingo campaigns to carry through to the arcade, but the colour change and the use of the light discs gives it it’s own personality.

User Testing

As part of a wider project ongoing in Bingo, we tested the Arcade prototypes with Bingo customers using various methods, such as remote and user lab testing.

This gave a good opportunity to get early feedback about the layout and design of Arcade, the homepage section dedicated to promoting the brand and the featured games and finally, the actual Arcade focused page.

The testing was to see if users understood what Arcade was, whether they would understand the difference between bingo and slot games and to see if there was an appetite to play them. We also tested whether the search and categorisation changes helped a user find their usual games once on the arcade page.

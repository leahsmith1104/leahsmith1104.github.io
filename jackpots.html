Jackpot Content

Client: Sky Betting and Gaming - Sky Vegas the is the UK's most popular online casino, it's the ultimate destination for new, exclusive, and exciting online slots. They bring the thrill of the unexpected to their players, wherever they choose to play. It’s where players can experience the anticipation of the unknown, and enjoy the buzz and excitement of playing big-name slots.

Project: Jackpot content (Jackpot King and Party Pots).

These are dedicated sections on the homepage to promote specific game content, which are tied together via jackpots. It came from a business need to promote jackpot games as exclusive content and a reason to get customers to continue playing with Sky Vegas. This was a cross product project including myself looking at the design and front end implementation, Product, Technology and Marketing.

Wireframes

Wireframes were created by UX as example placement of Jackpot King. The heirarchy of the section on site was signed off by stakeholders and engagement was tested in lab before being passed over to me to design and implement.

Design Concepts

Below are the initial designs that were pulled together for Jackpot King. These came from having the placement pretty much locked down by initial wireframe testing, but with a need to look at how best to convey the importance of jackpot size.

Final Designs: Jackpot King

The focus of the project was to incorporate the Jackpot king branding with game content to create a fun and exciting section that stands out from other content to attract the user’s attention. To do this I looked at centralising the brand of jackpot king and making sure the largest jackpot sum was visually the biggest, central and most prominent alongside the two smaller sums. A different font colour was used to make the jackpot amounts stand out from the titles and explanatory text. Finally, to make the section stand out from the normal content above and below, I included the jackpot king brand background and also a tapered border top and bottom. On mobile the background was lost as the site sits on a white, not dark background. Testing proved that a strip banner halted people scrolling any further down the page, which was not an objective.

Project: Party Pots

Party Pots followed on from the success of Jackpot king. It took the same placement on the homepage, but this time there were seven jackpots instead of three. Below is the styling of Party Pots, most of which was taken from the in game creative, and applied to the previous on site jackpot research.

Both jackpot sections are still live and can be found on Sky Vegas today.

Bonus Time

Client: Sky Betting and Gaming - Sky Vegas

Project: Sky Vegas Bonus time

As part of a business need to live the proposition of “Experts in the Unexpected”, the Bonus Time concept was created. Through research we knew that Sky Vegas struggled to be known for 'something' and that 'Bonus Rounds' and 'In-game rewards' were very important to customers.

A team was created to tackle this brief which included myself representing UI and Interaction, UX, Marketing, Brand, Creative, Product, Compliance and Media.

In the early design sprints, various stakeholders attended and outlined the objectives for Bonus Time and the overall requirements, which included updating the Homepage Hero area, creating the ‘sticky’ notifications to indicate when Bonus Time was active and a custom promotional page to give customers information about Bonus Time, how to play, what they can win etc.

My role was to focus on the UI and interaction. The main requirement for me was to highlight Bonus time on the homepage, this included the various states of active and inactive. And outline the key proposition on the detail page.

Wireframes

UX created and tested wireframes internally to ensure the overall user journey was correct and users would understand when Bonus Time was active, and where to find further information etc. These wires included various states of the homepage hero area, to announce Bonus Time was due to start and when it was active. Notifications were used to highlight when a bonus round was available in-game and the amount of rounds available onsite. Testing also took place on the promotion detail page to ensure the content was easy to understand and users could take away key information if required.

Outcomes of testing showed the journey was easy to understand; the states of the hero helped promote when Bonus Time was active and kept users engaged during playing sessions. The notifications kept users ‘in the loop’ about when Bonus rounds we’re happening, remaining round etc. and the promotion page housed clear, key information which was easy for new and existing customers to understand the Bonus Time concept.

Applying the branding

The final bonus time logo was provided by brand and aimed to carry through the importance of time with the use of cogs. After seeing the logo I used my initiative and added animation to capture the eye of any customer visiting Vegas and maybe tempt them into the bonus time detail page, or remind them about Bonus Time. This animation was similar to the ones used in external media, such as TV and Social Media.

Animation

As explained above, the cogs allowed for a great opportunity to encorporate some animation on site. especially with the different states depending on whether Bonus Time was active or not. At the top you can see the classic logo, below is the active logo. User Testing

As part of a wider project ongoing in Vegas, we tested the Bonus Time prototypes with Vegas customers using various methods, such as remote and user lab testing.

The aim of these methods was to gather as much insight as possible into how Vegas customers perceived Bonus Time as a concept and find if it would be something they would have an invested interest in. This testing showed customers had a positive response to the campaign concept and overall experience as a whole, whilst stating that the random chance of winning brought a huge sense of excitement and anticipation.

This also followed on from another outcome that our customers loves playing Slot games due to their unpredictability and sense that anything can happen, with Bonus Time carrying on this trend but offering chances of winning more lucrative prizes.
